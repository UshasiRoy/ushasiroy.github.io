About

Welcome to my academic website. I am a DST-INSPIRE Faculty Fellow at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Pune, in the Department of Physics. My research focuses on curiosity-driven research on The Physics of Living Systems, encompassing Stochastic Methods, Nonequilibrium Statistical Physics, Complex Systems, Soft Matter, and Quantitative Systems Biology.

My work bridges the gap between physics and biology, with particular emphasis on developing new computational and theoretical approaches for understanding spatiotemporal dynamics in biological systems. I collaborate with researchers in systems biology, soft matter physics, and computational biology to address fundamental questions about gene regulatory networks, cellular migration, and pattern formation.

Previously, I completed my Ph.D. from IIT Madras where I worked with Prof. Manoj Gopalakrishnan on "Intrinsic Noise in the Biochemical Sensory Pathway of Escherichia coli and its Implications." I also held postdoctoral positions at Purdue University (with Dr. Andrew Mugler) and IISc Bangalore (with Dr. Mohit Kumar Jolly).

Research

Spatiotemporal Dynamics of Gene Regulatory Networks

Investigation of how gene regulatory network motifs create spatiotemporal patterns in biological systems. This work explores the implications of inherent nonlinearities and stochasticities, with particular focus on genetic toggle switches and their role in cellular decision-making processes.

Computational Modeling of Multicellular Migration

Development of computational models to understand how cell-cell adhesion affects collective migration of multicellular clusters. This research has shown that intermediate adhesion maximizes migration velocity, providing insights into cancer metastasis and tissue development.

Stochastic Analysis of Bacterial Chemotaxis

Theoretical and computational studies of noise and fluctuations in bacterial chemotaxis networks, particularly in E. coli. This work employs path-integral formalism and master equation approaches to understand how biochemical noise affects bacterial navigation.

Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition in Cancer

Application of Random Matrix Theory and statistical physics approaches to characterize epithelial-mesenchymal transition during cancer metastatic invasion, focusing on understanding cellular plasticity and decision-making mechanisms.
